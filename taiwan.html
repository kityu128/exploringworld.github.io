Discover the Beauty of Taiwan

From bustling night markets to breathtaking mountain scenery, ancient temples to modern cities, Taiwan offers incredible diversity and unforgettable experiences.

Explore Destinations

Top Destinations in Taiwan

Taipei

Northern Taiwan

Taiwan's vibrant capital blends modern skyscrapers with traditional temples and some of Asia's best street food.

Must-See Attractions

  • Taipei 101 - Iconic bamboo-inspired skyscraper with observation deck
  • Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall - Impressive national monument and museum
  • Shilin Night Market - Taiwan's most famous night market with endless food options
  • Beitou Hot Springs - Relax in natural thermal waters just outside the city
  • Ximending - Youth shopping and entertainment district

Local Tip

Visit Elephant Mountain for the best views of Taipei 101 and the city skyline, especially at sunset.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (October-November) for pleasant weather. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter is mild but sometimes rainy.

Getting Around

The Taipei Metro (MRT) is efficient and covers most attractions. Get an EasyCard for convenient access to public transport. Taxis are affordable.

Taroko Gorge

Hualien County

One of Asia's most spectacular natural wonders with marble cliffs, winding roads, and hiking trails.

Must-See Attractions

  • Swallow Grotto Trail - Walk along sheer marble cliffs with the Liwu River below
  • Eternal Spring Shrine - Picturesque shrine built into the cliffside
  • Baiyang Waterfall Trail - Features a stunning waterfall and water curtain cave
  • Shakadang Trail - Easy walk along turquoise waters with beautiful rock formations
  • Qingshui Cliffs - Dramatic coastal cliffs where mountains meet the Pacific

Adventure Tip

For serious hikers, the Zhuilu Old Trail offers breathtaking views but requires a permit and has steep drop-offs.

Best Time to Visit

Year-round, but avoid typhoon season (July-September) when trails may close. Spring offers pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds.

Getting There

Take a train to Hualien (2-3 hours from Taipei), then a bus or taxi to Taroko. Consider renting a scooter for more flexibility within the park.

Tainan

Southern Taiwan

Taiwan's oldest city with rich history, traditional temples, and famous local snacks.

Must-See Attractions

  • Anping Old Fort - Historic Dutch fortress from the 17th century
  • Chihkan Tower - Former Dutch outpost turned Chinese-style pavilion
  • Shennong Street - Charming historic street with traditional houses
  • Confucius Temple - Taiwan's first Confucian temple, built in 1665
  • Flower Night Market - One of Taiwan's largest and most famous night markets

Foodie Paradise

Don't miss Tainan specialties like danzai noodles, coffin bread, milkfish soup, and shrimp rolls. Try them at local institutions like Du Hsiao Yueh.

Best Time to Visit

October to April when temperatures are milder. Avoid summer months which can be extremely hot and humid.

Getting Around

Tainan's bus system is improving but still limited. Taxis are affordable, and renting a bicycle is a great way to explore the flat city center.

Sun Moon Lake

Nantou County

Taiwan's largest lake surrounded by mountains, known for its scenic beauty and indigenous culture.

Must-See Attractions

  • Wenwu Temple - Grand temple overlooking the lake with three halls
  • Ita Thao Village - Indigenous Thao community with cultural performances
  • Ci'en Pagoda - Offers panoramic views from its hilltop location
  • Bike paths - Well-maintained cycling routes around the lake
  • Lalu Island - Small sacred island in the middle of the lake

Unique Experience

Take the Sun Moon Lake Ropeway for breathtaking aerial views, then visit Formosan Aboriginal Culture Village to learn about Taiwan's indigenous tribes.

Best Time to Visit

Spring and autumn for pleasant weather. Cherry blossoms appear in February-March. Avoid holiday weekends when it gets crowded.

Getting There

Take a bus from Taichung (about 1.5 hours). The Sun Moon Lake Pass offers unlimited bus rides and discounts on attractions.

Kaohsiung

Southern Taiwan

Taiwan's second largest city with a vibrant arts scene, night markets, and harbor views.

Must-See Attractions

  • Dome of Light - Stunning glass artwork in Formosa Boulevard MRT station
  • Pier-2 Art Center - Creative space in converted warehouses by the harbor
  • Liuhe Night Market - Famous for seafood and local specialties
  • Fo Guang Shan Buddha Museum - Massive Buddhist complex with a giant golden Buddha
  • Love River - Scenic waterfront perfect for evening strolls

Local Tip

Take the ferry to Cijin Island for fresh seafood, a black sand beach, and the Cihou Lighthouse with great city views.

Best Time to Visit

October to April when temperatures are milder. Summer can be extremely hot and humid.

Getting Around

Kaohsiung's MRT system is efficient. The city also has a good bike-sharing system (YouBike) and affordable taxis.

Alishan

Chiayi County

Mountain resort area famous for its sunrise views, sea of clouds, and ancient cypress forests.

Must-See Attractions

  • Sunrise at Zhushan - Spectacular dawn views from the observation deck
  • Alishan Forest Railway - Historic narrow-gauge train through scenic forests
  • Sacred Tree - Giant 3,000-year-old cypress (actually several trees)
  • Shouzhen Temple - Peaceful temple surrounded by cherry trees
  • Hiking trails - Well-maintained paths through ancient forests

Unique Experience

Stay overnight at one of the mountain hotels to experience the sunrise without the early morning rush from Chiayi.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-April) for cherry blossoms or autumn for clear skies. Winter can be chilly but offers the best chance for sea of clouds views.

Getting There

Take a train or bus to Chiayi, then the Alishan Forest Railway (2.5 hours) or bus (2 hours) up the mountain. The railway is scenic but often sells out.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (February-April)

Cherry blossom season at Alishan and Yangmingshan. Pingxi Sky Lantern Festival in February. Comfortable temperatures throughout Taiwan.

Summer (May-August)

Beach season in Kenting. Dragon Boat Festival in June. Hot and humid with occasional typhoons, but great for mountain escapes to cooler areas.

Autumn (September-November)

Pleasant weather ideal for hiking and city exploration. Moon Festival in September/October with barbecues and mooncakes. Fall foliage in higher elevations.

Winter (December-January)

Mild winters perfect for exploring cities. Hot spring season in Beitou and Wulai. Chinese New Year celebrations (dates vary).
