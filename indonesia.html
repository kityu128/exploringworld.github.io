Discover the Wonders of Indonesia

From the jungles of Sumatra to the beaches of Bali, the cultural riches of Java to the marine paradise of Raja Ampat, Indonesia offers endless adventures across its 17,000 islands.

Explore Destinations

Top Destinations in Indonesia

Bali

Lesser Sunda Islands

The Island of Gods, famous for its Hindu culture, stunning beaches, lush rice terraces, and vibrant arts scene.

Must-See Attractions

  • Uluwatu Temple - Cliffside sea temple with kecak fire dances at sunset
  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces - Iconic emerald-green stepped rice fields
  • Ubud - Cultural heart of Bali with art markets and monkey forest
  • Mount Batur - Active volcano with sunrise trekking opportunities
  • Nusa Penida - Stunning island with Kelingking Beach and manta ray snorkeling

Local Tip

Visit water temples like Tirta Empul for a traditional purification ritual. Go early to avoid crowds.

Best Time to Visit

April-October (dry season). July-August is peak season with higher prices. November-March is rainy but greener with fewer tourists.

Getting Around

Rent a scooter for flexibility or use ride-hailing apps like Grab. Private drivers are affordable for day trips. Traffic can be heavy in south Bali.

Komodo National Park

Flores, East Nusa Tenggara

Home of the legendary Komodo dragons and some of the world's best diving and pink sand beaches.

Must-See Attractions

  • Komodo Island - See the giant lizards in their natural habitat
  • Padar Island - Hike to the viewpoint over three stunning bays
  • Pink Beach - One of only seven pink sand beaches in the world
  • Manta Point - Snorkel with majestic manta rays
  • Kanawa Island - Pristine island with excellent snorkeling

Adventure Tip

Take a multi-day liveaboard boat trip to experience the park's remote islands and world-class dive sites like Batu Bolong.

Best Time to Visit

April-December (dry season). July-August has the calmest seas but most visitors. The park may close January-March during extreme weather.

Getting There

Fly to Labuan Bajo (Flores), then take a boat tour. Liveaboards range from basic to luxury. Day trips are available but limit what you can see.

Yogyakarta

Java

Java's cultural capital with ancient temples, royal palaces, and traditional arts.

Must-See Attractions

  • Borobudur - World's largest Buddhist temple at sunrise
  • Prambanan - Spectacular 9th-century Hindu temple complex
  • Kraton Palace - Sultan's royal palace with Javanese architecture
  • Malioboro Street - Vibrant shopping street with night market
  • Jomblang Cave - Underground cave with heavenly light beams

Cultural Experience

Watch the Ramayana ballet performance at Prambanan temple under the stars (seasonal).

Best Time to Visit

April-October (dry season). May-June has pleasant temperatures. Rainy season (Nov-Mar) can make temple visits muddy.

Getting Around

Hire a driver for day trips to Borobudur/Prambanan. Becak (cycle rickshaws) and taxis work for city transport. Motorbike rentals available.

Raja Ampat

West Papua

The world's most biodiverse marine environment with pristine coral reefs and stunning limestone islands.

Must-See Attractions

  • Wayag Islands - Iconic viewpoint over mushroom-shaped islands
  • Piaynemo - Another spectacular viewpoint with turquoise lagoons
  • Manta Sandy - Snorkel with manta rays in crystal clear water
  • Arborek Village - Traditional Papuan village with excellent house reef
  • Diving at Cape Kri - Holds the record for most fish species spotted on one dive

Diving Tip

October-April offers the best visibility and calmest seas. Bring an underwater camera - you'll see pygmy seahorses, wobbegongs, and more.

Best Time to Visit

October-April (dry season). July-September can be windy with rougher seas. May-June is transition period.

Getting There

Fly to Sorong via Jakarta/Makassar, then take a boat to islands like Waigeo or Misool. Liveaboards are popular for accessing remote sites.

Lombok

Lesser Sunda Islands

Bali's quieter neighbor with the majestic Mount Rinjani, stunning beaches, and traditional Sasak culture.

Must-See Attractions

  • Mount Rinjani - Indonesia's second highest volcano with crater lake
  • Gili Islands - Three car-free islands with great snorkeling
  • Sendang Gile Waterfall - Beautiful twin waterfalls in the jungle
  • Kuta Lombok - Stunning white sand beaches (not to be confused with Kuta Bali)
  • Sasak Villages - Traditional thatched roof villages like Sade

Adventure Tip

The 3-day Rinjani trek is challenging but rewarding. Book with a reputable company for proper equipment and guides.

Best Time to Visit

April-October (dry season). Rinjani is closed January-March due to heavy rains. May-June and September-October have fewer crowds.

Getting Around

Rent a scooter or hire drivers for longer distances. Fast boats connect to Bali and the Gilis. Public ferries are cheaper but slower.

Tana Toraja

South Sulawesi

Highland region known for unique funeral traditions, tongkonan houses, and spectacular landscapes.

Must-See Attractions

  • Londa Burial Caves - Cliffside graves with tau-tau effigies
  • Kete Kesu Village - Traditional village with tongkonan houses and rice barns
  • Lemo Graves - Rock-cut graves with balconies of tau-tau
  • Batutumonga - Scenic viewpoint over rice fields and valleys
  • Funeral Ceremonies - Elaborate multi-day events with buffalo sacrifices

Cultural Insight

Torajans believe death is a gradual process - bodies are kept at home for months/years until funds are raised for proper funeral rites.

Best Time to Visit

April-October (dry season). June-August is peak funeral season when most elaborate ceremonies occur.

Getting There

Fly to Makassar, then 8-10 hour drive to Rantepao. Hire a local guide to explain cultural significance of sites and arrange ceremony visits.

Seasonal Highlights

Dry Season (April-October)

Best time for beach vacations, diving, and trekking. Balinese Hindu holidays like Galungan and Nyepi (March/April) offer unique cultural experiences.

Rainy Season (November-March)

Fewer tourists, lush landscapes, and lower prices. Some areas may have transportation disruptions. Ideal for cultural visits in Java/Bali.

Festivals & Events

Waisak at Borobudur (May), Bali Arts Festival (June-July), Jakarta Fair (June-July), Toraja Funeral Ceremonies (peak June-August), and Ubud Writers & Readers Festival (October).

Diving Seasons

Raja Ampat best October-April, Komodo April-December, Bali year-round (manta season August-October). Alor has great visibility July-October.
