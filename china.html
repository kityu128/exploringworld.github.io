Must-Visit Destinations in China

Beijing

Northern China

China's capital blends ancient imperial grandeur with modern architectural marvels.

Must-See Attractions

  • The Great Wall - Mutianyu section offers stunning views with fewer crowds
  • Forbidden City - The world's largest imperial palace complex
  • Temple of Heaven - Where emperors prayed for good harvests
  • Summer Palace - Vast imperial garden with Kunming Lake
  • 798 Art Zone - Contemporary art in a converted factory complex

Local Tip

Visit the hutongs (ancient alleyways) by rickshaw to experience traditional Beijing life. Nanluoguxiang is the most famous but try quieter ones like Wudaoying.

Best Time to Visit

September-October for pleasant weather and clear skies. Spring (April-May) is also nice but can be windy. Winters are cold but less crowded.

Getting Around

Beijing's subway system is extensive and easy to use. Taxis are affordable but ensure drivers use the meter. Download Didi (Chinese Uber) for convenient rides.

Shanghai

Eastern China

China's most cosmopolitan city with futuristic skyline and historic charm.

Must-See Attractions

  • The Bund - Iconic waterfront with colonial architecture
  • Shanghai Tower - China's tallest building with observation deck
  • Yu Garden - Classical Chinese garden in Old Town
  • Tianzifang - Artsy lanes with boutiques and cafes
  • Zhujiajiao Water Town - "Shanghai's Venice" with canals

Food Experience

Try xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) at Jia Jia Tang Bao or Din Tai Fung. For local breakfast, sample shengjianbao (pan-fried buns) at Yang's Fry Dumplings.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather. Summers are hot and humid, winters chilly but manageable.

Transport Tips

The metro system is efficient and covers all major areas. Maglev train to Pudong Airport reaches 430km/h! Use Didi for taxis and download MetroMan app for subway maps.

Xi'an

Shaanxi Province

Ancient capital and starting point of the Silk Road, home to the Terracotta Warriors.

Must-See Attractions

  • Terracotta Army - Thousands of life-sized warrior statues
  • Ancient City Wall - Best preserved in China, cycle atop it
  • Muslim Quarter - Vibrant food street with Islamic influence
  • Big Wild Goose Pagoda - Buddhist landmark from Tang Dynasty
  • Shaanxi History Museum - Treasures from China's ancient capital

Local Experience

Try the famous Xi'an yangrou paomo (crumbled flatbread in mutton soup) in the Muslim Quarter. The best spots are usually the busiest ones with locals.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-October) offer pleasant temperatures. Summer can be hot, but the Terracotta Warriors museum is air-conditioned.

Getting Around

Xi'an has a good metro system and inexpensive taxis. The airport is about 40km northwest of the city center, connected by metro line 14.

Guilin

Guangxi Province

Famous for its karst mountain landscapes and the Li River, featured on the 20 yuan note.

Must-See Attractions

  • Li River Cruise - Scenic boat trip from Guilin to Yangshuo
  • Yangshuo - Picturesque countryside with rice paddies and karst peaks
  • Reed Flute Cave - Illuminated limestone cave with stalactites
  • Elephant Trunk Hill - Iconic riverside rock formation
  • Longji Rice Terraces - Stunning terraced fields of the Dragon's Backbone

Local Tip

For the best Li River experience, take the 4-hour cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo rather than the shorter options. The morning light makes for spectacular photography.

Best Time to Visit

April-May and September-October for pleasant weather. June-August is hot and humid but the landscapes are lush. Winter is mild but can be foggy.

Getting Around

Buses connect Guilin with Yangshuo and other nearby attractions. In Yangshuo, rent a bicycle or electric scooter to explore the countryside. Taxis are readily available in Guilin city.

Chengdu

Sichuan Province

Capital of Sichuan province, famous for pandas, spicy cuisine, and teahouse culture.

Must-See Attractions

  • Chengdu Panda Base - See giant pandas up close in a natural setting
  • Jinli Ancient Street - Traditional shopping street with Sichuan snacks
  • Wuhou Shrine - Temple dedicated to heroes of the Three Kingdoms
  • Qingcheng Mountain - Birthplace of Taoism with ancient temples
  • People's Park - Experience local teahouse culture

Food Experience

Don't miss Sichuan hot pot (try Huangcheng Laoma) and mapo tofu. For authentic local breakfast, try dandan noodles or spicy wontons at small street stalls.

Best Time to Visit

March-June and September-November for pleasant weather. Summers are hot and humid. Winter is mild but can be foggy.

Getting Around

Chengdu has an extensive metro system. Taxis are inexpensive. The airport is about 16km from downtown, connected by metro line 10.

Zhangjiajie

Hunan Province

Otherworldly sandstone pillars that inspired the floating mountains in Avatar.

Must-See Attractions

  • Zhangjiajie National Forest Park - Towering quartzite sandstone pillars
  • Avatar Hallelujah Mountain - The pillar that inspired Pandora's landscape
  • Tianmen Mountain - "Heaven's Gate" with glass walkways and cable car
  • Bailong Elevator - Glass elevator built into a cliff face
  • Golden Whip Stream - Scenic hiking trail through the park

Local Tip

Visit early in the morning to see the peaks emerging from mist. The best views are often after rain when clouds linger between the pillars.

Best Time to Visit

April-October, with September-October being ideal. Summers can be crowded. Winter offers fewer crowds but some trails may be closed.

Getting Around

Free shuttle buses operate within the national park. The city has taxis and buses. The airport has flights to major Chinese cities.

Chinese Culture & Traditions

🏮

Festivals

Experience Chinese New Year, Mid-Autumn Festival, Dragon Boat Festival and other vibrant celebrations with ancient traditions.

🎎

Performing Arts

Discover Peking Opera, acrobatics, shadow puppetry, and traditional music that date back centuries.

🎨

Arts & Crafts

Explore calligraphy, porcelain, silk embroidery, paper cutting, and other exquisite Chinese art forms.

☯

Philosophy

Learn about Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism - the three pillars of Chinese thought and spirituality.

Travel Essentials

Visa Requirements

Most visitors need a visa to enter China. Tourist visas (L visa) are typically valid for 30-90 days. Apply at your nearest Chinese embassy or consulate. Some cities offer 72/144-hour visa-free transit for certain nationalities.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) are generally the best times with pleasant weather across most regions. Summers can be hot and humid, especially in southern China. Winter offers fewer crowds but cold temperatures in the north.

Essential Apps

  • WeChat - All-in-one app for messaging, payments, and more
  • Didi - China's Uber equivalent for taxis
  • Pleco - Best Chinese-English dictionary app
  • MetroMan - Subway maps for major Chinese cities
  • VPN - To access Google, Facebook, etc. (install before arrival)
