Where East meets West - from ancient ruins to stunning beaches, vibrant bazaars to fairy-tale landscapes.

Top Destinations in Turkey

Istanbul

Marmara Region

The only city spanning two continents, where Byzantine, Ottoman, and modern Turkish cultures collide.

Must-See Attractions

  • Hagia Sophia - Architectural marvel that was church, mosque, and now museum
  • Blue Mosque - Stunning 17th-century mosque with six minarets
  • Topkapi Palace - Opulent home of Ottoman sultans for 400 years
  • Grand Bazaar - One of the world's oldest covered markets with 4,000 shops
  • Bosphorus Cruise - Boat ride between Europe and Asia

Local Tip

Visit mosques between prayer times and dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered). Women should bring a scarf for head covering.

Best Time to Visit

April-May and September-October for mild weather. July-August can be hot and crowded. Winters are chilly but magical with fewer tourists.

Getting Around

Istanbulkart for all public transport. Tram is best for Sultanahmet sights. Taxis can be expensive in traffic. Walking is great in historic areas.

Cappadocia

Central Anatolia

Surreal landscape of fairy chimneys, cave dwellings, and the world's most spectacular hot air balloon rides.

Must-See Attractions

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Sunrise flights over the magical landscape
  • Göreme Open-Air Museum - UNESCO site with rock-cut churches
  • Underground Cities - Kaymaklı or Derinkuyu's ancient subterranean cities
  • Love Valley - Famous for its phallic-shaped rock formations
  • Uçhisar Castle - Highest point in Cappadocia with panoramic views

Unique Experience

Stay in a cave hotel - many have been converted from ancient dwellings with modern comforts while preserving historic charm.

Best Time to Visit

April-June and September-October for pleasant weather. July-August can be hot. Winter offers snowy landscapes and lower prices.

Transport Tips

Fly to Kayseri or Nevşehir airports. Rent a car or join tours to explore the region. Local dolmuş (minibuses) connect main towns.

Ephesus

Aegean Region

One of the best-preserved ancient cities in the Mediterranean, with a history dating back to 10th century BC.

Must-See Attractions

  • Library of Celsus - Iconic two-story facade that's become Turkey's most photographed ancient structure
  • Great Theater - 25,000-seat theater where St. Paul once preached
  • Temple of Artemis - One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World (only foundations remain)
  • Terrace Houses - Well-preserved Roman homes with intricate mosaics and frescoes
  • House of Virgin Mary - Believed to be where Mary spent her final years

Historical Insight

Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and heat. The site has little shade, so bring sun protection.

Best Time to Visit

April-May and September-October for pleasant temperatures. Summer can be extremely hot. Winter is mild with few visitors.

Getting There

Closest airport is Izmir (1 hour drive). Selçuk is the nearest town with good bus connections. Many combine with a visit to Pamukkale.

Pamukkale

Aegean Region

Stunning white travertine terraces filled with thermal waters, nicknamed "Cotton Castle".

Must-See Attractions

  • Travertine Terraces - Walk barefoot on the warm, mineral-rich pools (bring a plastic bag for shoes)
  • Hierapolis - Ancient spa city ruins above the terraces
  • Antique Pool - Swim among ancient Roman columns in warm mineral water
  • Cleopatra's Pool - Legend says Cleopatra swam here (extra entrance fee)
  • Pamukkale Natural Park - Beautiful walking trails with views

Visitor Tip

The terraces are most photogenic in late afternoon light. Stay overnight to enjoy the terraces after day-trippers leave.

Best Time to Visit

April-May and September-October for pleasant weather. Summer can be extremely hot. Winter is mild but some pools may be closed.

Transport Tips

Denizli is the nearest city with airport and train station. Frequent dolmuş (minibuses) go to Pamukkale. Often combined with Ephesus visit.

Antalya

Mediterranean Coast

Turquoise coast gem with ancient ruins, stunning beaches, and the dramatic Taurus Mountains.

Must-See Attractions

  • Old Town (Kaleiçi) - Charming historic quarter with Ottoman houses
  • Düden Waterfalls - Spectacular falls cascading into the sea
  • Hadrian's Gate - Ornate Roman triumphal arch from 130 AD
  • Konyaaltı Beach - Long pebble beach with mountain views
  • Termessos - Dramatic mountaintop ancient city ruins

Day Trip Idea

Visit the ancient cities of Perge and Aspendos - the latter has one of the best-preserved Roman theaters still used for performances.

Best Time to Visit

May-June and September-October for beach weather without extreme heat. July-August is peak season. Winters are mild and quiet.

Getting Around

Antalya has good public buses and trams. Rent a car to explore nearby ancient sites. Boat trips along the coast are popular.

Bodrum

Aegean Coast

Stylish seaside resort with a medieval castle, vibrant nightlife, and beautiful beaches.

Must-See Attractions

  • Bodrum Castle - 15th-century Crusader castle housing the Museum of Underwater Archaeology
  • Bodrum Marina - Glamorous harbor filled with luxury yachts
  • Ancient Theater - Well-preserved Hellenistic theater with sea views
  • Bitez Beach - Popular windsurfing spot with shallow waters
  • Mausoleum at Halicarnassus - Site of one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

Boat Trip

Take a "blue cruise" to nearby coves and islands - options range from budget group boats to private gulet charters.

Best Time to Visit

May-June and September for pleasant weather and fewer crowds. July-August is peak season with nightlife. Winters are quiet but mild.

Transport Tips

Bodrum has an international airport. Local dolmuş (minibuses) connect beaches and towns. Walking is best in the peninsula center.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (April-May)

Ideal for Istanbul sightseeing and Cappadocia ballooning. Wildflowers bloom across Anatolia. Pleasant beach weather begins in Antalya.

Summer (June-August)

Peak beach season along Mediterranean and Aegean coasts. Hot but dry in Cappadocia. Istanbul festivals and outdoor dining.

Autumn (September-October)

Perfect weather nationwide. Grape harvest in Cappadocia. Sea still warm for swimming. Fewer crowds at ancient sites.

Winter (November-March)

Ski season in Uludağ and Palandöken. Istanbul is magical with fewer tourists. Cappadocia has snowy landscapes. Southern coast remains mild.
