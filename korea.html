Discover the Beauty of Korea

From ancient palaces to futuristic cities, cherry blossoms to stunning coastlines, Korea offers unforgettable experiences at every turn. Explore our curated selection of must-visit destinations.

Top Destinations in Korea

Seoul

Gyeonggi Region

Korea's dynamic capital blends cutting-edge technology with ancient traditions, from towering skyscrapers to historic palaces.

Must-See Attractions

  • Gyeongbokgung Palace - The largest of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces with changing of the guard ceremony
  • Bukchon Hanok Village - Traditional Korean houses (hanok) in a picturesque neighborhood
  • N Seoul Tower - Iconic tower offering panoramic city views and love locks
  • Myeongdong - Vibrant shopping district famous for street food and cosmetics
  • Dongdaemun Design Plaza - Futuristic landmark by Zaha Hadid with 24-hour shopping

Local Tip

Rent a hanbok (traditional Korean dress) to enter palaces for free and take stunning photos in the historic settings.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April) for cherry blossoms or autumn (October) for pleasant weather and fall colors. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters offer festive lights.

Getting Around

Seoul's subway system is extensive and easy to use. Get a T-money card for convenient travel on all public transport. Taxis are affordable but can get stuck in traffic.

Busan

South Gyeongsang

Korea's second-largest city and beach destination, famous for its seafood, film festival, and coastal scenery.

Must-See Attractions

  • Haeundae Beach - Most famous beach in Korea with luxury hotels and summer festivals
  • Gamcheon Culture Village - Colorful hillside neighborhood known as "Korea's Santorini"
  • Jagalchi Fish Market - Korea's largest seafood market where you can eat your purchase
  • Haedong Yonggungsa Temple - Stunning seaside Buddhist temple
  • BIFF Square - Center of Busan International Film Festival with street food stalls

Food Experience

Try Busan's famous milmyeon (cold wheat noodles), dwaeji gukbap (pork soup with rice), and fresh hoe (sliced raw fish) at Jagalchi Market.

Best Time to Visit

Summer (June-August) for beach activities, or October during the Busan International Film Festival. Spring and autumn offer pleasant weather with fewer crowds.

Transport Tips

Busan's subway covers most attractions. Consider the Busan City Pass for unlimited travel. Taxis are affordable for shorter distances.

Jeju Island

Jeju Province

Korea's premier holiday destination with volcanic landscapes, beaches, and unique cultural heritage.

Must-See Attractions

  • Hallasan - Korea's highest mountain with scenic hiking trails
  • Seongsan Ilchulbong - Sunrise Peak, a UNESCO-listed volcanic crater
  • Manjanggul Cave - One of the world's longest lava tubes
  • Jeongbang Waterfall - Only waterfall in Asia that falls directly into the ocean
  • O'Sulloc Tea Museum - Learn about Korea's green tea with beautiful plantations

Unique Experience

Visit a haenyeo (female diver) show to see these amazing free-diving grandmothers who harvest seafood without oxygen tanks.

Best Time to Visit

April-June and September-November for pleasant weather. July-August is peak beach season but crowded. February sees camellia flowers bloom.

Getting Around

Renting a car is ideal for exploring Jeju. Public buses cover main attractions but can be infrequent. Taxis are available but expensive for long distances.

Gyeongju

North Gyeongsang

Ancient capital of the Silla Kingdom, often called "the museum without walls" for its historical treasures.

Must-See Attractions

  • Bulguksa Temple - UNESCO World Heritage site and masterpiece of Buddhist art
  • Seokguram Grotto - Ancient stone Buddha with stunning coastal views
  • Daereungwon Tomb Complex - Large grassy mounds housing Silla kings
  • Cheomseongdae - Asia's oldest surviving astronomical observatory
  • Anapji Pond - Beautiful night illumination of reconstructed palace gardens

Cultural Tip

Visit Yangdong Folk Village nearby to see traditional Korean houses and experience rural life from the Joseon dynasty.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April) for cherry blossoms at Bomun Lake or autumn (October) for comfortable sightseeing. The Gyeongju Cherry Blossom Marathon in April is spectacular.

Transport Tips

Many sites are walkable in central Gyeongju. Buses connect to outlying attractions. Bicycles can be rented for exploring the historic areas.

Andong

North Gyeongsang

Center of Korean Confucian culture and home to the famous Hahoe Folk Village.

Must-See Attractions

  • Hahoe Folk Village - UNESCO-listed traditional village with mask dance performances
  • Andong Soju Museum - Learn about Korea's famous liquor with tastings
  • Woryeonggyo Bridge - Korea's longest wooden footbridge, beautifully lit at night
  • Bongjeongsa Temple - One of Korea's oldest wooden buildings
  • Andong Mask Dance Festival - Annual October event showcasing traditional performances

Food Specialty

Try Andong's famous jjimdak (braised chicken with vegetables) and heotjesabap (fake ceremonial rice meal with various side dishes).

Best Time to Visit

October during the Mask Dance Festival, or spring/autumn for pleasant weather. Summer can be hot and humid.

Getting There

Andong is accessible by train or bus from Seoul (about 3 hours). The Hahoe Folk Village is about 30 minutes by bus from Andong city center.

Incheon

Gyeonggi Region

Korea's third-largest city and major port, known for its Chinatown, futuristic Songdo district, and historic landing sites.

Must-See Attractions

  • Incheon Chinatown - Korea's only official Chinatown with colorful gates and jajangmyeon (black bean noodles)
  • Songdo International Business District - Futuristic smart city with Central Park and iconic architecture
  • Wolmido Island
    • - Seaside attraction with amusement park, boardwalk, and fresh seafood
  • Incheon Landing Operation Memorial Hall - Museum commemorating MacArthur's decisive Korean War operation
  • Eurwangni Beach - Popular beach near Incheon International Airport with sunset views

Food Experience

Try Incheon's famous jajangmyeon (black bean noodles) in Chinatown, and fresh seafood at Wolmido or Muuido Island.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) for pleasant weather. The Incheon Pentaport Rock Festival in August draws big crowds.

Transport Tips

Incheon is easily accessible from Seoul via subway (Line 1). The airport express train connects Incheon Airport to Seoul in 45 minutes. Buses serve all major attractions.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (March-May)

Cherry blossom festivals across Korea, with highlights at Jinhae (largest festival), Yeouido Park in Seoul, and Gyeongju. Also see canola flowers in Jeju and tea picking in Boseong.

Summer (June-August)

Beach season in Busan and Jeju, mud festivals in Boryeong, and monsoon rains (bring an umbrella!). Try patbingsu (shaved ice dessert) to beat the heat.

Autumn (September-November)

Stunning fall foliage, especially in Seoraksan National Park, Naejangsan, and the gardens of Seoul. Chuseok (Korean Thanksgiving) brings traditional ceremonies.

Winter (December-February)

Ski resorts like Yongpyong and Alpensia, ice fishing in Hwacheon, and festive lights at Garden of Morning Calm. Try warm winter street foods like hotteok (sweet pancakes).
