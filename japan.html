Discover the Beauty of Japan

From ancient temples to futuristic cities, cherry blossoms to snow monkeys, Japan offers unforgettable experiences at every turn. Explore our curated selection of must-visit destinations.

Top Destinations in Japan

Tokyo

Kanto Region

Japan's bustling capital blends ultramodern with traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples.

Must-See Attractions

  • Shibuya Crossing - The world's busiest pedestrian scramble with up to 3,000 people crossing at once
  • Senso-ji Temple - Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple in Asakusa with a vibrant Nakamise shopping street
  • Meiji Shrine - Serene Shinto shrine nestled in a forest near Harajuku
  • Tokyo Skytree - The world's tallest freestanding broadcasting tower with observation decks
  • Tsukiji Outer Market - Vibrant market with fresh seafood and sushi restaurants

Local Tip

Visit teamLab Planets Tokyo for an immersive digital art experience - book tickets in advance for this popular attraction.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (late March to April) for cherry blossoms or autumn (October to November) for pleasant weather and fall colors. June brings rainy season, while summer can be hot and humid.

Getting Around

The Tokyo Metro and JR lines cover the entire city. Get a Suica or Pasmo IC card for easy tap-and-go access to all trains and buses. Taxis are expensive but convenient late at night.

Kyoto

Kansai Region

Japan's cultural heart with over 2,000 temples and shrines, preserving traditional arts and architecture.

Must-See Attractions

  • Fushimi Inari Taisha - Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates forming mountain trails
  • Kinkaku-ji - The stunning Golden Pavilion covered in gold leaf
  • Kiyomizu-dera - Wooden temple with panoramic views from its "stage"
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - Magical walking path through towering bamboo
  • Gion District - Kyoto's famous geisha district with preserved machiya houses

Cultural Experience

Participate in a tea ceremony at Camellia Flower or En tea house to experience this traditional art form firsthand.

Seasonal Highlights

April for cherry blossoms (especially Philosopher's Path), November for autumn foliage (Kiyomizu-dera is spectacular), and July for Gion Matsuri festival with massive floats.

Transport Tips

Kyoto's bus system reaches most attractions. Consider renting a bicycle for local exploration. The Kyoto City Subway has two main lines connecting major areas.

Osaka

Kansai Region

Known as "Japan's Kitchen" for its incredible street food, vibrant nightlife, and friendly locals.

Must-See Attractions

  • Osaka Castle - Iconic symbol of the city with a museum inside and beautiful grounds
  • Dotonbori - Neon-lit entertainment district famous for its street food and giant crab sign
  • Universal Studios Japan - Popular theme park with Wizarding World of Harry Potter
  • Umeda Sky Building - Floating Garden Observatory with 360° city views
  • Shinsekai - Retro-futuristic district with Tsutenkaku Tower and kushikatsu

Foodie Paradise

Don't miss takoyaki (octopus balls) at Wanaka Honten, okonomiyaki at Mizuno, and kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) in Shinsekai.

Best Time to Visit

Spring and autumn for pleasant weather. Summer brings Tenjin Matsuri (July) with fireworks and boat processions. Winter illuminations are spectacular.

Getting Around

The Osaka Amazing Pass offers unlimited subway/bus rides plus free entry to many attractions. Osaka's subway system is efficient and covers all major areas.

Hiroshima

Chugoku Region

A city of peace with moving memorials to its tragic past and nearby natural beauty.

Must-See Attractions

  • Peace Memorial Park - Powerful tribute to atomic bomb victims with museum and Atomic Bomb Dome
  • Miyajima Island - Home to the "floating" torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine
  • Hiroshima Castle - Reconstructed feudal-era castle with informative museum
  • Shukkei-en Garden - Beautiful traditional Japanese garden dating to 1620
  • Mazda Museum - Fascinating look at this iconic car manufacturer

Local Specialty

Try Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki - layered with noodles and more ingredients than the Osaka version. Recommended spot: Okonomi-mura.

Best Time to Visit

Spring for cherry blossoms at Peace Park or autumn for fall foliage on Miyajima. August 6th is Peace Memorial Day with moving ceremonies.

Transport Tips

Miyajima is accessible by train and ferry (covered by JR Pass). Hiroshima's tram system is convenient for city travel. Consider the Hiroshima Tourist Pass for unlimited transport.

Nara

Kansai Region

Japan's first permanent capital, home to ancient temples and friendly deer roaming freely.

Must-See Attractions

  • Todai-ji Temple - Houses the Great Buddha (Daibutsu), one of Japan's largest bronze statues
  • Nara Park - Hundreds of sacred deer that bow for special deer crackers
  • Kasuga Taisha - Famous for its hundreds of bronze and stone lanterns
  • Kofuku-ji Temple - Five-story pagoda that's a symbol of Nara
  • Isuien Garden - Beautiful traditional garden with teahouses

Unique Experience

Buy shika senbei (deer crackers) from vendors and bow to the deer - they may bow back! Be careful as they can get pushy when hungry.

Best Time to Visit

Year-round, but especially beautiful during cherry blossom season (late March-early April) or autumn foliage (November). February brings the unique Omizutori fire festival.

Getting There

Nara makes an easy day trip from Kyoto or Osaka (about 45 minutes by train). The main sights are walkable from Kintetsu Nara Station. Buses also available.

Hakone

Kanagawa Prefecture

Famous for hot springs, natural beauty, and spectacular views of Mount Fuji.

Must-See Attractions

  • Hakone Open-Air Museum - Stunning outdoor sculpture park with mountain views
  • Lake Ashi - Take a pirate ship cruise with potential Fuji views
  • Hakone Ropeway - Gondola with breathtaking volcanic valley views
  • Owakudani - Active volcanic valley with sulfur vents and black eggs
  • Onsen (hot springs) - Relax in therapeutic waters at ryokan or public baths

Ryokan Stay

For the full experience, stay at a traditional ryokan like Gora Kadan or Hakone Ginyu, featuring kaiseki meals and private onsen baths.

Best Time to Visit

Clear days in winter offer the best views of Mount Fuji. Autumn is beautiful for foliage. Avoid rainy season (June) when views may be obscured.

Transport Tips

The Hakone Free Pass covers all transport (trains, buses, ropeway, pirate ship) and offers discounts. The Romancecar train from Shinjuku is a scenic option.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (March-May)

Cherry blossom viewing (hanami) is the quintessential Japanese experience. Best spots include Ueno Park (Tokyo), Philosopher's Path (Kyoto), and Hirosaki Castle (Aomori).

Summer (June-August)

Enjoy vibrant festivals like Gion Matsuri in Kyoto and Nebuta Matsuri in Aomori. Hike the scenic trails of the Japanese Alps or relax on Okinawa's beautiful beaches.

Autumn (September-November)

Fall foliage transforms Japan into a colorful wonderland. Top viewing spots are Nikko, Kyoto's temples, and the Japanese gardens throughout the country.

Winter (December-February)

Ski in world-class resorts like Niseko, see snow monkeys bathing in hot springs in Nagano, or experience illuminations in Tokyo and Osaka.
